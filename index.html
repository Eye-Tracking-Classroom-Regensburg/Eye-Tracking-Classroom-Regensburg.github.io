The Eye-Tracking Laboratory on campus of the University of Regensburg

Since 2019, the Ostbayerische Technische Hochschule and the University of Regensburg operate a joint Eye-Tracking-Laboratory on campus in Regensburg. In the facility, which is equipped with the latest technology, research is conducted on new findings in the field of user behavior and interaction with modern digital systems. The 14 interconnected high-performance eye trackers are an unique selling point. By networking these devices, new research areas are opened up, especially regarding the investigation and support of collaborative tasks through gaze data acquisition.

Partners

The laboratory is operated collaboratively by LaS 3, the Chair of Paedagogy III and the Chair of Media Informatics and queues a long series of joint and successful research projects of this group.

Prof. Dr. Jürgen Mottok, LaS 3, OTH Regensburg

Prof. Dr. Jürgen Mottok is the head of LaS 3 and and also, since December 2016, holds the ZD.B research professorship "Sichere und zuverlässige dezentrale Systeme" (engl. „safe and reliable decentralised systems“). He has been pursuing the subject of eye tracking since 2015 and is particularly interested in how it can be used in the context of quality assurance in software projects.

Prof. Dr. Dr. h.c. Hans Gruber, Chair of Pedagogy III

Dr. Hans Gruber is the holder of the Chair of Pedagogy III and has been working with the use of eye tracking in expertise research since 2013. His areas of interest in relation to eye tracking focus on visual expertise in various domains (e.g. medicine, art or computer science).

Prof. Dr. Christian Wolff, Chair of Media Informatics

Christian Wolff was appointed to the Chair of Media Informatics in 2010. Equipment

The Tobii Spectrum 600 eye trackers used in the laboratory are connected to modern workstations. The workstations reside within the same local network and thus allow parallel recording and real-time streaming of gaze data from multipleusers. In addition to opening up new fields of research, this also enables a more efficient way to conduct classical eye-tracking studies with large samples. Especially, educational studies profit from a higher ecological validity due to the classroom setup.

Alongside a separate observation area, the main laboratory area is oriented towards classical learning/teaching environments. Consequently, the majority of the systems is installed there. This setup allows the in situ observation and evaluation of teaching situations, e.g. the didactic sharing of content in a classroom. The setup is complemented by specially adapted head-mounted displays, which allow the examination of gaze data in virtual environments. Additional workstations in the observation area enable the evaluation of recorded experiments parallel to laboratory operations.

The entire equipment of the eye-tracking laboratory was funded by the FHInvest project "Eyes on Future". This BMBF-funded initiative is specifically intended to expand the research-relevant infrastructure at German universities and to create new research and cooperation opportunities.

Research

The special equipment of the laboratory enables the investigation of a wide range of topics that reflect the mutual as well as varying research interests of the partners involved. Key objectives are the investigation and support of user behavior in the context of software development, the improvement of learning and teaching situations, as well as the investigation and integration of new interaction modalities in digital systems.

Recent studies

The hardware of the eye tracking laboratory has already been utilised in three studies. Security aspects of e-mail clients, the reading behavior of programmers in C ++ reviews and anomalies in medical and hospital hygiene were examined. Future planned projects deal with the different forms of representation of code during the software development life cycle, visual expertise in radiologists and teachers‘ eye movements during class.

Software Engineering Education in Co-located Multi-User Eye-Tracking-Environments

This tutorial explores the application of Eye-Tracking technology for Software Engineering education. We show a research gap concerning its usage for real- time improvement of traditional learning scenarios and outline a number of activities to generate research ideas and prototypical solutions to close this gap. We describe our expertise as active researchers in the relevant fields and propose a possible format to document the outcomes of this workshop for further studies.
